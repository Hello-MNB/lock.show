# **B4-35.30 — DESIGN SYSTEM & TRUST UX COMPONENT STANDARD**

## *Production design-system authority*

  

## *DOCUMENT CONTROL*

Version: v1.7

Date: 2 July 2026

Status: CURRENT — SPECIFIED FOUNDATION; VISUAL DIRECTION NOT YET SELECTED; IMPLEMENTATION AND ENVIRONMENT ACCEPTANCE PENDING

Owner/accountable: R13 — Principal Product Designer, Design Systems & Trust UX Lead

Subordinate to ★ B4 Master Canon; SSOT for components, semantic tokens and Trust UX only.

Technology implementation truth: governed outside folder 35 by the current Technology build-state authority.

  

1\. SYSTEM PURPOSE

Create one reusable, accessible and privacy-safe interface system for Artist Radar, Bookability Passport, management-agency workspace, demand-professional, Source Confirmer and operator experiences. A component is not accepted because it matches a colour palette; behavior, states, workspace and role context, Hebrew RTL and English LTR parity, locale switching, mixed-script safety, accessibility, permissions and failure recovery are part of the component contract.

  

2\. FOUNDATIONS

Primary canvas: \#0A0A0B.

Primary surface: \#15151A.

Primary accent: lime \#B4D900 for the single dominant action and approved strong state where semantically valid.

Secondary accent: teal \#1EE6C8 for secondary emphasis and developing state.

Text: high-contrast near-white on dark; muted neutral for secondary information.

Missing evidence: restrained amber/orange.

Not assessable / disabled: neutral grey.

Typeface: Heebo is the working dual-script Hebrew/Latin UI typeface until visual-direction selection. Hebrew and English must receive equal typographic hierarchy and optical quality. Russian and German glyph coverage remains scaffolded and must be verified before Phase-2 activation; this sentence does not constitute a native-editor or release pass. Arabic remains user-content fallback only unless separately approved as a locale.

Body minimum: 16 px. Tap target minimum: 44×44 px. Mobile design reference: 360 px.

Spacing, radius, elevation and motion must be implemented as semantic tokens, not repeated literals.

  

3\. SEMANTIC TOKEN MODEL

Required token families:

• colour.background, colour.surface, colour.text, colour.border;

• colour.action.primary, secondary, destructive, disabled;

• colour.status.supported, partial, missing, notAssessable, stale, restricted;

• type.display, heading, body, label, helper, code;

• space.1–12; radius.1–5; elevation.0–4; motion.fast, standard, slow;

• focus.ring, error.outline, privacy.private, privacy.public, privacy.internal.

Token names remain semantic across themes and personas. No component may encode business meaning through raw hex values alone.

  

4\. GLOBAL INTERACTION RULES

• One clearly dominant action per task state or bounded panel. Operational dashboards may contain multiple actions when they are visibly ranked and do not compete as equal primaries.

• Progressive disclosure rather than stacked long forms.

• Preserve entered data after recoverable failure.

• Every empty or error state explains the next action.

• Deep links never switch role or organization silently.

• Public, private, restricted and internal data are visually and structurally distinct.

• Save, reaction, request, endorsement and booking intent must not be visually conflated.

• Destructive, publication, permission, payment and operator actions require explicit consequence language.

  

5\. PHASED LOCALE, DIRECTION, RESPONSIVE AND ACCESSIBILITY

EN + HE are Phase-1 locales. RU + DE are Phase-2 locales: their technical scaffolds may exist earlier, but they are not shipped until complete Translation Matrix coverage, functional parity, QA and native-editor review pass. Hebrew is authored native-first. English is the development and StringKey baseline only, not the source language. The distinction אמרגן ≠ מפיק is preserved in every user-facing string. Hebrew uses native RTL; English, Russian and German use native LTR. Locale choice persists and switching never loses route, form state or progress. Navigation, alignment, breadcrumbs, progress, tables, drawers and spacing respond to the active locale and direction.

Mixed-language names and source text use correct bidirectional isolation and never reorder IDs, ILS/NIS values, dates, phone numbers or links. The Translation Matrix records coverage and native-QA status per locale. Feature flags may scaffold RU + DE before Phase 2, but neither locale may be activated for users or shipped before its release gates pass.

Active beta journeys target WCAG 2.2 AA: semantic headings, labelled controls, keyboard completion, visible focus, screen-reader names, contrast, reduced motion and error association.

Artist and Source Confirmer flows must be fully completable at 360 px. Bookability Passport actions must work at 360 px and desktop. Agency/operator tables collapse to labelled cards without losing meaning or actions.

  

6\. TRUST UX FIREWALL

Permanently prohibited on every public and private surface:

• overall score, rank, percentile, probability or superiority claim;

• radar, spider, web, polygon, circular multi-axis or gauge scoring visual;

• generic verified badge without the exact method;

• public display of raw evidence, private values, internal confidence, gaps or recommendations;

• any visual implying followers equal draw;

• synthetic or demonstration data that can be mistaken for real evidence.

Artist Radar remains the product name; the visual prohibition does not rename the product.

  

7\. CORE COMPONENT CONTRACTS

7.1 ProofUnit

Anatomy: Claim → Context → MethodLabel → ReviewedDate.

Optional: bounded public band, source owner category, expandable limitation.

Never: bare checkmark, generic verified status, raw evidence or unsupported certainty.

  

7.2 BoundedStatusChip

Allowed private states: well supported, partially supported, evidence missing, not assessable, stale/needs refresh, restricted.

Private Radar and authorized operational contexts only. Technically incapable of mounting in public Passport output as an artist-worth label.

  

7.3 PrimaryAction

One visible dominant action. Minimum 44 px target, explicit verb, loading/disabled/success/error states and preserved input.

  

7.4 ProfessionalActionSheet

Opened by the single Bookability Passport CTA: Check availability.

Visual hierarchy: one principal request option; other bounded options are secondary, diagnostic or close actions. Request price/details is inside the sheet, not a second Passport CTA.

Writes a ProfessionalReaction activity event for every explicit action; Save additionally writes a durable private SavedPassport state; AvailabilityRequest is written only for commercial request actions.

  

7.5 RoleContextSwitcher

Shows active role, organization and access state. Supports explicit switch, expired/revoked recovery and zero sensitive counts from another context.

  

7.6 ConsentBlock

Four separate consent purposes total: privacy processing; public publication; third-party evidence; optional marketing. No bundle, pre-check, dark pattern or conditional ambiguity.

  

7.7 PermissionStateBanner and InvitationCard

States: sent, accepted, declined, expired, revoked, suspended, disputed, limited. Every state explains the next permitted action without revealing private record existence.

  

7.8 EvidenceCapture and Upload

Claim-first prompt, file/source type, owner authority, consent, date, privacy and retention. States include validation, upload, processing, delayed, failed, retry, withdrawn and replacement. Failed processing never deletes the artifact.

  

7.9 ConfirmationTask

Displays exact artist, event, statement, requester, proposed use and privacy notice. Actions: confirm, correct, decline, cannot assess. No endorsement pressure. Provides receipt and withdrawal/correction route.

  

7.10 PassportRenderer

Preview and public use the same renderer and immutable PassportVersion. Public output includes only passport-ok artist-approved claims. Server-side publication validation requires at least two supported Proof Units including at least one live-demand or commercial-evidence unit. Identity, biography, media and streaming/social context alone cannot qualify. Omit weak/empty rows. Media failure cannot block proof.

  

7.11 OfferCard, PaymentState and EntitlementState

Render price, currency, tax, period and included capabilities only from OfferVersion. Client cannot grant entitlement. States: offered, accepted, payment submitted, review pending, approved, rejected, activated, expired, refunded/revoked.

  

7.12 FeedbackLauncher and IncorrectInformationReport

General feedback and content/privacy reporting are distinct routes. Both capture safe page, role and release context. Screenshots are optional and private.

  

7.13 OperatorReasonDialog

High-risk operator actions require actor, reason, confirmation, timestamp and audit. Raw evidence is hidden by default.

  

7A. DISCOVERY-CANDIDATE UI STUB

DiscoveryCandidateCard — private artist-workspace component for the consent-gated fourth-door flow: surface → artist confirms, rejects or edits.

Shows: found candidate value, candidate type, source URL/source label, discovery date and current review state.

Actions: Confirm, Reject, Edit. Confirm may create a Claim only through the approved server-side transition and method label; Edit remains unconfirmed until a later explicit Confirm action.

Never: auto-publish, create any Claim before explicit confirmation, imply verification, count an unconfirmed or edited-pending candidate as evidence, or expose it on the Bookability Passport.

  

8\. PERSONA DENSITY

Artist: guided, reassuring, mobile-first, one next move.

Passport: editorial, artist-led, premium, proof explicit but not dashboard-dense.

Agency Roster Radar: management-agency workspace interface; roster-capable, no rankings, with separate team, role-context and ArtistAccess indicators. "Roster Intelligence" may appear as the interface descriptor.

Demand professional: fast, restrained, decision-oriented, no feed or recommendation aesthetic.

Source Confirmer: neutral, bounded, single task.

Operator: higher density allowed for queues; status colours and internal metadata never leak to public surfaces.

Applicability fairness — N/A ≠ ZERO. A field not applicable to the artist's type is removed, never rendered as a gap or weakness. Applicability is controlled by the artist-taxonomy and field-applicability authority, not hard-coded persona assumptions. Taxonomy values and final columns remain UNDER DISCOVERY — not yet decided; do not build or freeze.

SPECIFIED → DESIGNED → CODED → ENVIRONMENT-VERIFIED → RELEASE-ACCEPTED.

11\. DESIGN ACCEPTANCE

Fail when role context is ambiguous, permissions leak metadata, entered data is lost, a prohibited score visual appears, public/private content differs unexpectedly, a second Bookability Passport primary CTA appears, copy hard-codes tax/price, locale switching loses state, HE/EN content meaning diverges, or a critical journey fails 360 px mobile Hebrew RTL, 360 px mobile English LTR, mixed-script safety, keyboard or screen-reader completion. Discovery also fails acceptance if an unconfirmed or edited-pending candidate creates a Claim, appears as evidence or reaches a public Passport.

  

12\. ACTIVE DESIGN EXECUTION — B4-DES-002

R13 must first establish one coherent product language through three anchor experiences before the system is propagated across the full screen inventory.

  

Authenticated anchors resolve through person → workspace → role. Agency is a workspace type, not a role. Agency Roster Radar is the management-agency workspace interface; it operates only within the active workspace, role and ArtistAccess scope. The public Bookability Passport remains a public artifact and does not require a viewer workspace.

  

Anchor experiences:

• Artist Radar home — mobile-first at the 360 px reference, Hebrew-native RTL + EN LTR; guided personal intelligence, one dominant next action, evidence freshness and non-shaming progress.

• Public Bookability Passport — mobile-first at the 360 px reference, Hebrew-native RTL + EN LTR; premium artist-led proof, Attributed Draw hierarchy, visible method and one CTA.

• Agency Roster Radar — management-agency workspace interface, mobile-first at the 360 px reference with a desktop operational view, Hebrew-native RTL + EN LTR; operational credibility, active workspace and role context, roster density and clear ArtistAccess state.

  

Visual directions to be explored on the same anchors:

A — Editorial Proof Intelligence: premium editorial hierarchy, strong artist presence and quiet evidence cards.

B — Professional Intelligence System: precise grid, restrained analytical structure and explicit context/permissions.

C — Live Intelligence: credible live-entertainment energy, cinematic artist presence and disciplined evidence presentation without nightlife-flyer styling.

  

Decision rule: these are controlled explorations, not three separate design systems. Bilingual shell, direction behavior and visual exploration may proceed in parallel with Product clarification on stable anchor fixtures; product-dependent interaction semantics remain provisional. Maria selects the strategic direction after reviewing the same HE/EN anchor content, device conditions and component specimens. R13 records the selected direction here, retires rejected directions as exploration assets and then builds the reusable component library.

  

Until selection, the current dark canvas, lime/teal accents and Heebo typography are a working foundation—not final visual approval. Engineering must not broadly beautify or propagate a visual direction before R13 records Maria's selection in this document.

  

Required anchor component specimens: ProofUnit, MethodLabel, BoundedStatusChip, PrimaryAction, ProfessionalActionSheet, RoleContextSwitcher, ConsentBlock, PermissionStateBanner, EvidenceCapture, DiscoveryCandidateCard and Source Confirmer ConfirmationTask.
