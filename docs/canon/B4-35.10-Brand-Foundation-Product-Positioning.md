1\. CATEGORY  
GIGPROOF is consent-based pre-booking intelligence and trust infrastructure for live music. It sits before booking operations: Artist Radar turns lawful evidence into private diagnosis, freshness and next actions; the Bookability Passport turns artist-approved supported strengths into buyer-facing decision intelligence; professional actions and missing-proof signals feed the learning loop.

2\. CORE PROBLEM  
Talent does not automatically create bookability. A talented artist may lack self-marketing capability, structured proof and access to the offline relationships through which bookings are made. A booking professional risks reputation, time and event economics when recommending an unfamiliar artist without explainable evidence.

3\. PRODUCT ARCHITECTURE  
Artist Radar — private recurring artist intelligence and improvement product.  
Mirror — the current-state reflective module inside Artist Radar; never the replacement product.  
Bookability Passport — curated, artist-approved, buyer-facing decision artifact. It requires at least two supported Proof Units, including at least one live-demand or commercial-evidence unit; identity, biography, media and streaming/social context alone never qualify.  
Attributed Draw — context-specific evidence of audience or commercial pull, expressed only through supportable bands, binary signals or bounded statements with a named method and reviewed date.

4\. ONE-LINE POSITIONING  
English: GIGPROOF turns fragmented live-performance evidence into method-labelled pre-booking intelligence, helping professionals evaluate unfamiliar artists before they risk a recommendation, shortlist or booking decision.

Product-locale decision: English, Hebrew, Russian and German are current product locales. English is the first implementation and source-language baseline. Hebrew uses native RTL; English, Russian and German use native LTR. The Translation Matrix governs semantic parity, native review and activation. No locale is forced solely by country, and Russian/German are not moved outside current scope merely because they follow English in implementation order.

5\. VALUE BY PARTICIPANT  
Artist: turn existing activity, assets and evidence into a clearer professional position, prioritized next actions and an approved Passport.  
Booking manager / agent: reduce time and reputational risk when considering an unfamiliar artist.  
Producer / promoter: inspect context-specific evidence without reconstructing it across calls, screenshots and social profiles.  
Venue programmer / artistic director: assess relevance, activity and operational readiness, then route the artist to the correct decision-maker.  
Agency / artist manager: standardize roster proof while preserving artist ownership, permissions and publication control.  
Source Confirmer: confirm or correct one bounded statement without being asked to endorse the artist.

6\. DIFFERENTIATION  
• Supported draw, not follower theatre.  
• Method-labelled evidence, not generic verification badges.  
• Local and event context, not vanity reach.  
• Private diagnosis, buyer-facing decision intelligence and observable professional actions from one controlled evidence system.  
• Artist-approved publication and privacy-safe bands.  
• Pre-booking decision support, not booking CRM, contracts, settlement or event operations.  
• Permissioned evidence history as a potential moat; defensibility remains a thesis until proven through real use.

7\. BRAND PRINCIPLES  
Credible. Clear. Calm. Decisive. Human. Scene-literate. Premium without exaggeration.  
There is no universal intelligence-versus-culture percentage. The balance is surface-specific: Artist Radar and Bookability Passport require strong artist presence, performance energy and live-scene credibility while keeping proof legible; agency, professional and operator surfaces may be more system-dense. The shared territory is Live Intelligence—show energy disciplined by evidence, never sterile enterprise SaaS and never nightlife-flyer spectacle.

8\. TRUST PRINCIPLES  
Trust is created by visible method, context, recency, honest missing states, artist control and stable product behavior—not by colour, polish or a bare checkmark.  
Every public Proof Unit follows:  
Claim → Context → Method label → Reviewed date.

9\. POSITIONING FIREWALL  
Never claim or imply:  
• artist score, rank, percentile or probability;  
• guaranteed booking, room fill or commercial outcome;  
• universal “verified artist” status;  
• that social followers or streaming listeners equal ticket demand;  
• independent verification when the evidence is artist-declared or artifact-supported;  
• that GIGPROOF replaces taste, relationships or professional judgment;  
• that the category, payer, price, moat or product-market fit is proven before accepted evidence exists.

10\. VISUAL TERRITORY  
Use Live Intelligence: editorial artist presence, cinematic performance energy, visible proof hierarchy, method labels and spacious mobile-first layouts. Emotional impact should lead on artist-facing and public surfaces; operational clarity should lead on agency and operator surfaces.  
Avoid generic blue-SaaS aesthetics, sterile compliance dashboards, party clichés, nightlife-flyer styling, gamified progress and competitive humiliation.  
The name Artist Radar is approved. Radar charts, spider charts, polygon charts, circular multi-axis charts and gauge-like scoring visuals are permanently prohibited on public and private surfaces.

11\. PRODUCT BOUNDARIES  
Current bounded beta includes the complete required multi-sided system: artist, agency, demand professional, Source Confirmer, public viewer and operator. Gate-1 is the first implementation and validation slice, not the only product scope.  
Explicitly deferred without separate authorization: marketplace, global artist discovery, automatic matching, ranking, booking probability, full booking CRM, negotiation, holds, contracts, settlement, tour logistics and automated commission rails.

12\. EVIDENCE-STATUS LANGUAGE  
Use: working thesis, candidate, under test, pilot signal, observed action, artist-declared, artifact-supported, producer-confirmed, source reviewed on \[date\], priced rejection, paid conversion.  
Avoid: market proven, independently verified, guaranteed draw, universal benchmark, validated category after one conversation.

13\. SUCCESS TEST  
Can GIGPROOF transform real, obtainable artist evidence into privacy-safe pre-booking intelligence and a credible Bookability Passport that a skeptical professional understands and acts on—and will a defined payer pay for the versioned offer?
