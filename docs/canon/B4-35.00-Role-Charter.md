# 1\. ROLE PURPOSE

  

The Principal Product Designer, Design Systems & Trust UX Lead is accountable for converting GIGPROOF's product, evidence, role, privacy and commercial requirements into a coherent, accessible and testable experience across every required participant.

  

The role protects the venture from six design failure modes:

  

• Product drift — the interface changes the approved product architecture or reduces Artist Radar to a Passport builder.

• Trust drift — evidence appears more certain, verified or predictive than its source supports.

• Context failure — users cannot tell which role, organization, artist or permission context they are acting under.

• Usability failure — a real participant cannot complete the intended task without founder or operator rescue.

• System failure — one-off screens, visual inconsistencies and undocumented states create implementation and QA debt.

• Validation failure — visual preference, page views or generic praise are reported as product or money proof.

  

# 2\. GOVERNING EXPERIENCE QUESTION

  

Can every participant understand, without hidden explanation:

  

• where they are;

• which role and organization they are acting under;

• what information is private, public, restricted or internal;

• what a displayed Claim proves and does not prove;

• how that Claim was supported and when it was reviewed;

• what action is available now;

• what consequence the action creates;

• how to recover from error, interruption, expiry, revocation or dispute?

  

# 3\. ROLE CHARTER

  

The role creates a controlled experience system in which:

  

• Artist Radar remains the private artist intelligence and improvement product.

• Mirror is a reflective module or state inside Artist Radar, not a replacement product.

• Passport is a curated, artist-approved, buyer-facing proof artifact.

• Attributed Draw remains method-labelled, contextual and privacy-safe.

• No public or private interface presents a score, rank, percentile, probability, guarantee or disguised artist-worth rating.

• No polygon, spider, radial or gauge visual is used as a scoring metaphor.

• Role, organization, membership and ArtistAccess remain explicit and separate.

• Consent, publication, confirmation, correction, withdrawal and payment states are understandable before commitment.

• Each screen has one dominant user decision and one primary CTA.

• The current product architecture contains four locales: English, Hebrew, Russian and German. English is implemented first as the source-language baseline; Hebrew uses native RTL and English/Russian/German use native LTR. The Translation Matrix controls coverage, native review and activation. Implementation order never removes a locale from current scope, and no locale is forced solely by country. Arabic is not a current interface locale; user-entered Arabic remains safely isolated.

• Every critical journey supports empty, loading, partial, stale, offline, error, forbidden and recovery states.

• Design specifications map to reusable coded components and measurable acceptance criteria.

• Design quality is verified in the declared environment, not inferred from files.

Define navigation, role-context visibility, progressive disclosure, task sequence, state transitions, recovery, cross-role handoffs and the boundary between public, authenticated, restricted and operator experiences.

  

## 4.2 Artist Radar Experience

  

Protect and design Artist Radar as the primary private recurring artist experience.

  

Required experience areas include:

  

• professional overview and one dominant next move;

• identity and positioning;

• bookable offer;

• biography and professional narrative;

• professional footprint, assets and channels;

• evidence and Attributed Draw;

• commercial and authority readiness;

• technical readiness;

• opportunity-specific pitch;

• T01–T10 tasks;

• Passport readiness;

• freshness, progress and post-performance learning;

• proposed cohort context only after Maria approval and only where sufficient lawful and methodologically valid data exists.

  

Use bounded states, directional context and actionable gaps. Cohort comparison remains pending Maria approval and must not be implemented or presented as current scope.

  

## 4.3 Passport and Trust UX

  

Own the buyer-facing evidence hierarchy and the reusable Proof Unit:

  

Claim → Context → Method label → Reviewed date

  

Ensure that the Passport:

  

• keeps the artist visually and professionally central;

• makes Attributed Draw legible within the first decision window;

• shows only artist-approved, supportable public strengths;

• omits weak or unsupported public rows rather than displaying public failure;

• separates streaming and social context from evidence of local draw;

• never exposes raw evidence, exact confidential values, internal confidence, reasoning, gaps, recommendations or private contacts;

• uses one primary action entry, with bounded professional actions inside the next step;

• distinguishes view, save, reaction, request, forward and booking intent.

  

## 4.4 Design System

  

Own a production design system, not only a palette or reference document.

  

The system must include:

  

• semantic design tokens;

• typography, spacing, radius, elevation and motion scales;

• responsive and mobile-first behavior;

• English LTR layout and mixed-script isolation behavior;

• component anatomy, properties, variants and states;

• interaction, focus, keyboard and screen-reader behavior;

• form, upload, validation and recovery patterns;

• permission, invitation, dispute, consent and entitlement patterns;

• public, artist, agency, professional and operator density rules;

• visual-regression specimens;

• mapping between design tokens and implemented React/Tailwind components;

• component status: specified, designed, coded, environment-verified and release-accepted.

  

## 4.5 Trust, Privacy and Permission Patterns

  

Own the UX expression of approved controls for:

  

• private/public/internal visibility;

• artist publication control;

• active role and organization context;

• organization membership versus ArtistAccess;

• invitation, acceptance, expiry, revocation and dispute;

• Source Confirmer confirmation, correction, decline, inability to assess and withdrawal;

• incorrect-information and privacy reporting;

• processing, publication, third-party evidence and optional marketing consent;

• OfferVersion, price, payer, payment and entitlement state;

• high-risk operator actions with reason and audit;

• immutable Passport versions, unpublish and rollback.

  

The role implements approved legal and technical requirements in the experience. It does not provide legal approval or technical certification.

  

## 4.6 Accessibility, Localization and Low-Digital-Confidence UX

  

Own experience acceptance for:

  

• English, Hebrew, Russian and German at 360 px and above, with Hebrew RTL and the other three locales LTR;

• correct native RTL/LTR layout, locale switching and bidirectional isolation for mixed user-entered content;

• accessible contrast, focus and semantic structure;

• keyboard and screen-reader completion;

• minimum target sizes and reduced motion;

• plain native Hebrew and professional English, short sentences and a clear action hierarchy;

• resumable tasks and preserved input after error;

• non-shaming empty, missing-evidence and recovery states;

• exact role language appropriate to the participant's actual risk and authority.

  

Accessibility target: WCAG 2.2 AA for active beta journeys unless a documented restriction is approved.

  

## 4.7 Product Research and Validation Design

  

Define role-specific:

  

• prototype and usability tests;

• comprehension tasks;

• evidence-method trust tests;

• field observation scripts;

• beta task scripts;

• behavioral events;

• issue severity;

• experiment variants;

• acceptance thresholds.

  

Keep separate:

  

• visual preference;

• usability and task completion;

• evidence comprehension;

• trust;

• meaningful professional action;

• repeated product use;

• willingness to pay.

  

A page view, positive comment, like, emoji or "I would use this" is not a proven app or money signal.

  

## 4.8 Design Release Assurance

  

Issue one design verdict for each reviewed release:

  

• APPROVED;

• APPROVED WITH RESTRICTIONS;

• BLOCKED.

  

Design acceptance requires implementation evidence in the declared environment. A screen is not accepted because it matches colors or a Figma frame.

## 6.3 Trust UX Veto

  

The role may block design acceptance when:

  

• a score, percentile, rank, probability, gauge, polygon or disguised competitive rating appears;

• a public Claim lacks method, context or reviewed date where required;

• private Radar information can appear in Passport output;

• active role or organization context is ambiguous;

• save, request, endorsement and booking intent can be confused;

• permission denial reveals private record existence or metadata;

• consent is bundled, pre-checked, coercive or unclear;

• payment or entitlement state can be inferred or changed only in the client;

• synthetic or demonstration evidence can be mistaken for real evidence;

• entered information is lost after a recoverable error;

• a critical journey fails Hebrew RTL or English LTR, locale parity, mixed-script safety, mobile, keyboard or screen-reader completion;

• an unverified capability is presented as live, automated, secure or validated.

  

# 7\. OPERATING BOUNDARIES

  

Product and Strategy: The role may challenge hierarchy, comprehension, sequencing, user burden and product drift. It may not redefine GIGPROOF, choose the target persona, select the payer or declare product-market fit.

  

Technology and Engineering: The role defines experience behavior, component contracts, accessibility and design acceptance. It may not certify RLS, secrets, infrastructure, AI accuracy, deployment or security.

  

Legal and Privacy: The role designs understandable consent, rights, disclosure and withdrawal experiences from approved requirements. It may not provide legal advice or counsel approval.

  

Finance and Commercial: The role designs OfferVersion, payment and entitlement comprehension. It may not set price, VAT, refunds, forecasts or commercial terms.

  

PMM and Brand: The role owns in-product hierarchy and UX language together with Product Language. PMM owns external market messaging. Maria retains final brand authority.

  

Research and Sales: The role defines test design and evidence capture. Research and Sales recruit participants and own accepted market evidence. The role may not turn one opinion into a validated market claim.

  

Operations and Support: The role designs exception, dispute and support experiences. It does not automatically own daily evidence review, payment approval, support or incident response.

  

Founder: Maria retains final authority over product, brand, pricing, launch, budget and accepted risk. The role must make unresolved design and trust risk explicit and must never relabel a blocked state as approved.

# 9\. OPERATING PROCESS

  

The role operates through one controlled loop:

  

1\. Align — confirm current authority, persona, problem, evidence status and release scope.

2\. Specify — define journey, information hierarchy, states, component contracts and acceptance.

3\. Design — produce testable flows and reusable system patterns.

4\. Implement — hand off bounded component and screen contracts to Engineering.

5\. Verify — review the real environment with real states, permissions, Hebrew RTL, English LTR, locale switching, mixed-script safety, accessibility and failure paths.

6\. Validate — observe role-specific task completion, trust comprehension and meaningful action.

7\. Correct — update current authorities in place; classify defect, misunderstanding, missing requirement or future opportunity.

8\. Release — issue a written design verdict tied to release version.

9\. Learn — route accepted findings to the accountable current document without creating disconnected reports.

• artist self-completion;

• Passport published, shared, unpublished or refreshed;

• explicit professional reactions;

• requests stored and delivered;

• confirmations completed or corrected;

• post-performance closeouts and recurring Radar use.

  

### Commercial behavior:

  

• exact OfferVersion viewed;

• price and free alternative understood;

• payment initiated and approved;

• entitlement activated and used;

• priced rejection reason;

• repeat purchase, refresh or subscription behavior.

  

# 11\. DESIGN DEFINITION OF DONE

  

A critical journey is design-done only when:

  

• the approved persona and job are explicit;

• route, role and organization context are clear;

• every state and boundary is specified;

• content follows current Product Language;

• the flow uses reusable design-system components;

• every activated locale works at 360 px; English-first implementation remains compatible with Hebrew RTL and English/Russian/German LTR; locale switching preserves task state and mixed-script user content remains isolated;

• keyboard, focus and screen-reader behavior pass;

• empty, loading, partial, stale, error, forbidden and recovery states pass;

• no score, ranking, prediction, guarantee or unsupported trust mark appears;

• private, public and internal data remain visually and structurally distinct;

• analytics distinguish view, save, reaction, request and outcome;

• implementation is reviewed in the declared environment;

• S0/S1 design defects are closed and accepted S2 restrictions are documented;

• the release has a written design verdict.
