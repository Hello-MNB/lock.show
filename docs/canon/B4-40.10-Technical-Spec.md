## 2.3 ACTIVE-DISCOVERY FIREWALL AND ARTIST-AGNOSTIC LAW

The Artist Radar is an ACTIVE discovery tool (surfaces candidates), not only a passive display. Discovery→artist-confirms→method-label is the sole path web data may enter. SURFACE, never PUBLISH-as-fact.

The schema is ARTIST-AGNOSTIC. No table, column, enum, or rule may assume a specific artist type, genre, platform, or that any particular evidence exists (no assumption of Spotify, of ticket draw, of a catalogue, of a DJ format). Artist type is expressed ONLY through the taxonomy + field\_applicability tables, which switch fields on/off per type via N/A≠ZERO. Any hardcoded type-specific logic is a defect. The Radar must discover and structure information for ANY artist on one engine.

## 4.1 LAYER 3 — ACTIVE DISCOVERY TARGET SCHEMA (TARGET DESIGN; NOT MIGRATIONS)

This section is TARGET schema design, NOT migrations.

  

discovery\_candidate — 🔢 GATE-1 (concierge)

# 5\. EVIDENCE, PROVENANCE AND CLAIM-SUPPORT MODEL

GIGPROOF does not verify artists globally. It evaluates whether a source supports one narrow Claim.

  

Required four-operation model:

1\. ACQUIRE — receive an upload, approved public reference, official connected-source response, counterparty confirmation or an artist-confirmed discovery\_candidate. An unconfirmed discovery\_candidate remains outside Claim support.

2\. EXTRACT — read the factual content visible in the source.

3\. ATTRIBUTE — match it to the correct Act, event, date, territory, source owner and context.

4\. CLAIM-SUPPORT — apply explicit rules to determine what the source proves and does not prove.

  

A successful upload, OCR result or AI extraction does not make a Claim verified.

  

## 5.1 Supported evidence paths

• Artist or authorized representative upload.

• Artist-approved public URL.

• Official OAuth/API connection where available and permitted.

• Producer/source confirmation through a controlled request.

• Artist-confirmed discovery: a consent-gated discovery\_candidate may become a Claim only after explicit artist confirmation; before confirmation it is not evidence, not a Claim and not Passport-eligible.

• Honest manual self-report.

  

The product must remain functional through upload and self-report when external APIs are unavailable.

  

## 5.2 Source-support examples

Ticket export:

Supports tickets issued, sold or attributed exactly as shown.

Does not prove that every attendee came because of the artist.

  

Settlement or payment artifact:

Supports payment, guarantee, settlement or attendance fields actually present.

Does not prove future draw.

  

Producer confirmation:

Supports only the exact statement and event context confirmed.

Does not create a general endorsement or prove access to complete books.

  

Public lineup or event record:

Supports appearance, event, date, venue and listed role.

Does not prove ticket contribution.

  

Streaming or social profile:

Supports public footprint at review time.

Does not prove local paid demand.

  

Screenshot:

Supports that visible information was submitted and extracted.

Authenticity and context may remain supporting or self-reported.

  

Owned-community evidence:

Supports an aggregate size/band and artist control at the review date.

Member lists and member PII are prohibited.

  

## 5.3 Claim state dimensions

Do not overload one "verified" field. Store separately:

  

Provenance / support:

counterparty-confirmed · artifact-supported · artist-declared · unable-to-assess

Technical discovery certainty: SELF\_CONFIRMED\_FROM\_WEB applies only after explicit artist confirmation of a discovery\_candidate. It is not counterparty confirmation, artifact support or a public "verified" label; user-facing wording remains governed by B4-35.20.

  

Freshness:

fresh · aging · stale

  

Lifecycle:

submitted · processing · awaiting-artist-review · approved · published · disputed · withdrawn · superseded

  

Publication:

private · approved-for-publication · published · withdrawn

  

Internal confidence and ambiguity flags may route review but must never appear as quality, ranking, percentile or booking probability.

  

## 5.4 Public Proof Unit

Every public Claim must render as one controlled Proof Unit:

• Claim wording.

• Band or binary where permitted.

• Event/territory/time context.

• Artist role at event.

• Method label.

• Reviewed date.

• Expandable "supports / does not establish."

• Source-confirmation or correction state where relevant.

  

Public output may not include:

• Overall score, meter, gauge, rank or percentile.

• Booking prediction or guarantee.

• Exact restricted headcount, fee, payment or private commercial value.

• Private gaps, rejection reasons or internal confidence.

• Blanket "verified artist" badge.

# 6A.4 Processing flow

# 1\. COLLECT — obtain consented profile, assets, evidence and source snapshots.

# 2\. NORMALIZE — map lawful inputs to approved genre/style, region, maturity, act-format, live-context, offer, readiness, evidence-coverage and freshness taxonomies.

# 3\. ELIGIBILITY FILTER — deterministic rules remove ineligible, stale, disallowed or insufficiently supported records.

# 4\. CANDIDATE PROPOSAL — AI may propose semantically similar artists or cohort candidates from the eligible pool.

# 5\. RULE VALIDATION — deterministic rules accept, reject or route each candidate for review and record the reason.

# 6\. COHORT SUFFICIENCY — apply the approved minimum cohort size, missing-data tolerance, source-coverage and suppression policy.

# 7\. BOUNDED COMPARISON — generate descriptive patterns, strengths, gaps and next actions without rank, score, percentile, prediction or guarantee.

# 8\. EXPLANATION — every output states the comparison basis, source dates, method version, limitations and why the action was suggested.

# 9\. ARTIST REVIEW — the artist may correct inputs, reject candidates, opt out, dismiss an action or request deletion.

# 10\. PASSPORT CANDIDATES — supported strengths may be offered for separate artist review and public publication; comparison output itself never crosses to the Passport.
