1\. GLOBAL SCREEN CONTRACT

Every screen states the persona, active role and organization context, job, entry and exit, route, records read and written, fields, visibility, permissions, consent dependency, components, actions, state transitions, analytics, mobile/desktop behavior, accessibility and acceptance.

Contract structure: one canonical Screen Contract schema, one route-to-Screen-ID register and shared permission, invitation, state/recovery and analytics dictionaries are authoritative. Individual cards contain screen-specific values and exceptions rather than repeating or silently redefining shared rules.

Every user-facing screen is specified for four current product locales: English, Hebrew, Russian and German. English is the first implementation and source-language baseline. Hebrew uses native RTL; English, Russian and German use native LTR. An explicit user preference persists; no locale is forced solely by country. Switching locale preserves route, active role, organization and artist context, entered data, drafts and recoverable progress. Internal identifiers remain locale-neutral. The Translation Matrix records coverage, review and activation status for every screen and state.

Every critical screen supports applicable empty, loading, partial, stale, validation, network, permission, expired/revoked, deleted/withdrawn and recovery states. Recoverable failure never erases saved input.

  

2\. GLOBAL TRUST RULES

• No score, rank, percentile, booking probability, guarantee or superiority claim.

• No radar, spider, polygon, circular multi-axis or gauge scoring visual.

• Proof Unit = Claim + Context + Method label + Reviewed date.

• Artist Radar is the private recurring workspace; Mirror is its overview state.

• Passport is a strength-only, artist-approved public artifact.

• Streaming/social context never proves local draw by itself.

• Public output excludes raw evidence, exact confidential values, internal confidence, gaps, recommendations, private contacts and unrelated requests.

• One sticky Passport CTA opens the professional action sheet.

• Price, currency, tax and entitlements come from OfferVersion.

• Four consent purposes total: privacy processing; public publication; contextual third-party evidence; optional marketing.

  

3\. PERSONA-CONTEXT COVERAGE

The bounded beta distinguishes eleven explicit contexts even where screens or components are shared: independent artist; represented artist; artist manager acting personally; agency owner or administrator; agency team member; booking manager or agent; producer or promoter; venue programmer or artistic director; Source Confirmer; public Passport viewer; GIGPROOF operator. A Person may hold multiple contexts, but each journey retains a distinct job, authority, data boundary, completion condition and recovery model.

  

4\. PERSONA 1 — ARTIST

A1 — Account Entry

Job: enter the correct approved workspace and context.

Reads: Person, RoleAssignment, OrganizationMembership and beta access state.

States: default, loading, invalid input, unverified state, expired session, restricted access and recovery.

Acceptance: no field loss; correct context routing; every activated locale works at 360 px; Hebrew RTL and English/Russian/German LTR; Translation Matrix parity; state-preserving locale switch; keyboard and screen-reader completion.

  

A2 — Role and Context Setup

Route: /select-role and /choose-context.

Job: select the functional role and establish the correct solo or organization context.

Reads: Person, RoleAssignment, OrganizationMembership, ArtistAccess and current ActiveRoleContext.

Writes: ActiveRoleContext only after explicit selection and successful permission validation.

Rules: one person may hold multiple roles; selecting a RoleAssignment never grants OrganizationMembership or ArtistAccess. OrganizationMembership controls organization capability only. ArtistAccess independently controls artist-private access and carries explicit scope, status and effective period. A deep link may offer a context switch but never performs one silently.

States: no eligible role; one eligible context; multiple contexts; membership pending; ArtistAccess pending; expired; revoked; suspended; disputed; deleted organization; network failure; retry and safe return.

Recovery: preserve lawful user-owned draft input, return to the last valid context or context selector and reveal no unauthorized artist or organization metadata.

Analytics: role\_context\_selected; organization\_context\_selected; context\_switch\_blocked; context\_recovery\_completed.

Acceptance: switching never merges permissions or data; a user without ArtistAccess cannot read artist-private records even when they are an organization member.

  

A3 — Consent Gate

Job: show and record the R11-approved notice, permission or consent required for the specific processing purpose before the relevant write.

Record: ConsentRecord with purpose, policy version, status, timestamp, source and withdrawal state.

Purposes: privacy processing; public publication; contextual third-party evidence; optional marketing.

Rules: four purposes total; separate unchecked controls; public-publication consent appears at the Radar-to-Passport transition; marketing is optional.

Acceptance: the screen follows the R11-approved lawful basis and wording; where consent is required, declining blocks only the relevant write; optional marketing never blocks service; withdrawal consequences are explained and applied.

  

A4 — Goal-First Onboarding

Job: understand the artist's immediate professional objective and prioritize the evidence journey without changing what is true.

Writes: ArtistGoal and onboarding progress.

Options: build first Passport; prepare for an opportunity; strengthen draw proof; organize the professional footprint; refresh old evidence.

Acceptance: goal affects sequence and guidance, never Claim support rules.

  

A5 — Artist Identity

Job: capture the professional identity used across Radar and Passport.

Writes: Act, ArtistProfile and MediaAsset references.

Fields: stage name, optional private legal name, genre/style taxonomy, base, territories, one-line positioning, biography, languages, photo, public links and contact routing.

Visibility: each field is private, Passport candidate or internal.

Acceptance: no public publish without artist selection; mobile Hebrew RTL and English LTR, locale parity and media fallback pass.

  

A6 — Gig Activity

Job: record one real performance or event context.

Writes: Gig, VenueReference and SourceReference.

Fields: event, date, venue, region, lineup role, event type, capacity band where known, producer/promoter, public reference and private notes.

Rules: unknown is allowed; public appearance does not automatically prove draw.

Acceptance: duplicates can be resolved; records can be corrected or withdrawn.

  

A7 — Claim-First Evidence Capture

Job: start from what the artist wants to prove, then request matching evidence.

Writes: EvidenceArtifact, SourceReference and processing status.

Evidence purposes: attributed draw; ticket activity; fee/guarantee band; owned community; repeat booking; professional activity; releases/credibility; admin/technical readiness.

Fields: related gig/Claim, artifact or approved source, source owner, date, authority to use, visibility, retention and withdrawal.

States: draft, upload, validating, processing, delayed, failed, retry, ready, withdrawn and replaced.

Acceptance: private by default; failed processing retains the original input; unnecessary third-party personal data is not stored.

  

A8 — Claim Review

Job: approve, correct, reject or hide each proposed Claim before it influences Radar or Passport.

Reads: Claim, ClaimSupport, artifact metadata and proposed public representation.

Shows: buyer-readable wording, permitted private value, public band where relevant, method, support status, reviewed date, freshness and visibility.

Actions: approve, edit, reject, hide, replace source and request reprocessing.

Acceptance: AI never publishes; unsupported facts remain artist-declared or not assessable; every accepted Claim traces to permitted support.

  

A9 — Artist Radar

Job: explain the current evidence position, why gaps matter and the next useful action.

Reads: approved Claims, evidence coverage, activity, readiness, freshness, prior actions and permitted professional reactions.

Areas: overview; identity/positioning; bookable offer; biography; assets/channels; Attributed Draw; commercial/authority readiness; technical readiness; opportunity pitch; T01–T10 tasks; Passport readiness; progress/history; Gig Evidence Refresh.

Display: linear cards, bounded statuses, method/freshness and one prioritized action; never aggregate score or score-shaped visual.

PROPOSAL BOUNDARY — Similar-Artist / Cohort Comparison is pending Maria approval and is not approved build scope. The following sentence is retained only as decision material.

Cohort context: suppressed until lawful source rules, minimum cohort policy and method validation exist. At small N show absolute observations, never percentages.

Acceptance: artist can understand what is known, missing, why it matters and what to do next without founder explanation.

  

A10 — Passport Selection and Publication Consent

Job: choose which supported strengths cross from Radar to public Passport.

Reads: passport-ok Claim candidates.

Writes: selected Claim references, publication ConsentRecord and draft PassportVersion.

Rules: unsupported, hidden, withdrawn, expired or private-only Claims cannot be selected. Consent is separate and unchecked.

Acceptance: artist can remove any candidate; no public state before explicit publication.

  

A11 — Passport Readiness

Job: show exact blockers to a credible and safe public artifact.

Checks: identity minimum; at least two supported Proof Units; at least one live-demand or commercial-evidence Proof Unit; method labels; reviewed dates; public media; contact routing; privacy; publication consent; active entitlement where required. Identity, biography, media and streaming/social context alone never satisfy publication readiness.

States: ready, ready with omitted optional sections, blocked with actions and stale refresh required.

Acceptance: no low-score language; blockers link directly to corrective tasks.

  

A12 — Offer

Job: explain the active commercial offer and free alternative without ambiguity.

Reads: OfferVersion and eligibility.

Shows: offer name, payer, price, currency, tax treatment, period, included capabilities, exclusions, free alternative and expiry.

Actions: accept, decline, ask a question or continue with the free alternative.

Acceptance: no hard-coded price or tax; exact OfferVersion stored with exposure and response; priced rejection remains distinct from payment.

  

A13 — Payment and Entitlement Status

Job: submit approved payment evidence and understand review status.

Writes: payment reference; entitlement is never created by client action alone.

Fields: OfferVersion, payer, amount/currency from evidence, payment-method category, submitted artifact and note.

States: draft, submitted, review pending, correction required, approved, rejected, cancelled and refunded/revoked.

Acceptance: entitlement activates only through the approved server-side path; the artist receives a clear receipt and next step.

  

A14 — Passport Preview

Job: view the exact public artifact before publication.

Reads: draft PassportVersion through the public renderer.

Checks: no private fields, no internal statuses, no unsupported rows, one CTA, media fallbacks, share preview and draft indexing protection.

Actions: return to edit or approve and publish.

Acceptance: preview/public parity is tested against the immutable version.

  

A15 — Public Passport

Job: let a relevant professional understand one believable, method-backed proof story within 30 seconds.

Hierarchy: artist identity; Attributed Draw Proof Units; track record; catalogue/credibility; audience context; booking/admin readiness; supported references; one sticky CTA Check availability.

States: high evidence, mixed evidence, minimal honest evidence, media unavailable, unpublished, not found and slow connection.

Rules: empty proof rows are omitted; strengths only; streaming/social remains context; page is forwardable without private-data leakage.

Analytics: view, meaningful proof visibility, method opened, music opened, action sheet opened, explicit reaction and request outcome are distinct events.

Acceptance: persisted PassportVersion; no hard-coded artist data; signed-in and public viewers receive the same proof content.

  

A16 — Share Hub

Job: create and manage share links without implying recipient surveillance.

Writes: ShareLink with PassportVersion, campaign label, creator and status.

Shows: copy link, approved WhatsApp share text, active/disabled state and aggregate privacy-safe results.

Rules: no public recipient identity; tagged links do not change proof content.

Acceptance: revoked or unpublished Passport becomes safely unavailable.

  

A17 — Gig Evidence Refresh

Job: capture fresh post-performance evidence through a bounded update flow.

Writes: Gig update, new EvidenceArtifact, supported DrawSignal candidate and freshness event.

Fields: event context, ticket/settlement evidence if available, source reference, repeat-booking signal and artist notes.

Boundary: not booking pipeline, negotiation, relationship manager, settlement tool or event CRM.

Acceptance: evidence becomes reviewable and refreshes Radar only after artist review.

  

5\. PERSONA 2 — BOOKING MANAGER / DEMAND PROFESSIONAL

  

B1 — Passport Evaluation

Uses the shared A15 renderer. The professional can inspect Proof Units, methods, track record, readiness and music without account creation.

Acceptance: 30-second comprehension task; correct distinction between supported draw and social context; no hidden ranking.

  

B2 — Professional Action Sheet

Route: /passport/:id/react; commercial continuation /passport/:id/request.

Job: record one explicit professional action without forcing every action into a commercial request.

Entry: the single Passport CTA.

Active context: anonymous public recipient or authenticated demand professional acting personally or through an explicitly selected organization.

Primary commercial actions: check availability; request price/details.

Secondary actions: save for review; forward to decision-maker; future fit.

Diagnostic action: request specific proof.

Close action: not fit for this event.

Writes: ProfessionalReaction for every submitted action. Save also writes a private durable SavedPassport record. AvailabilityRequest is written only for the two commercial actions.

AvailabilityRequest fields: PassportVersion, artist, requester, selected organization/role where supplied, event date/type/location, capacity/budget bands if supplied, message, return channel and status.

Rules: save is private and is not endorsement or commercial intent; forward does not identify or surveil the recipient; diagnostic feedback is not commercial intent; no action is a booking, hold, negotiation, contract or settlement.

States: default; anonymous; authenticated personal context; authenticated organization context; validation error; duplicate request; delivery pending; delivery failed; withdrawn request; unavailable Passport; revoked organization capability and retry.

Recovery: preserve entered request data through sign-in, context selection and retry; retry is idempotent and cannot create duplicate requests.

Analytics: action\_sheet\_opened; action\_selected; passport\_saved; professional\_reaction\_created; availability\_request\_submitted; availability\_request\_withdrawn; delivery\_failed; delivery\_recovered.

Acceptance: each action has distinct persistence, analytics and downstream routing; save never creates AvailabilityRequest; only commercial actions enter artist or agency request handling.

  

B3 — Request Receipt

Job: confirm the exact action, artist, return channel and next expected step.

States: submitted, duplicate detected, delivery pending, delivery failed with recovery and optional verified account attachment.

Acceptance: no double submission; public-form progress survives a later sign-in attempt.

  

B4 — Professional Workspace

Routes: /professional/onboarding, /professional, /professional/saved, /professional/requests, /professional/requests/:id, /professional/notes/:passportId, /professional/notifications and /professional/settings.

Job: return to privately saved Passports, own or authorized-organization requests and private notes without creating an artist-discovery product.

Reads: active Person, RoleAssignment, ActiveRoleContext, OrganizationMembership where selected, SavedPassport, permitted AvailabilityRequest, Notification and private note records.

Writes: SavedPassport state, private note, permitted request-status update and notification preferences.

Boundary: only Passports opened by valid link and explicitly saved, plus own or authorized-organization requests. No marketplace, recommendation feed, hidden data, cross-organization history, negotiation or booking CRM.

States: first use; no saved Passports; no requests; loading; stale PassportVersion; deleted/unpublished Passport; revoked membership; forbidden organization request; note-save failure; network failure and recovery.

Recovery: switch explicitly to a valid context, retain unsaved note text during recoverable failure and return to the nearest permitted list without exposing inaccessible record metadata.

Analytics: professional\_home\_opened; saved\_passport\_opened; private\_note\_saved; request\_history\_opened; request\_status\_updated; context\_recovery\_completed.

Acceptance: active context is visible; removal or revocation takes effect immediately; private notes never reach the artist or public renderer; organization history is visible only with current capability.

  

B5 — Evidence Feedback / No-Fit

Job: capture missing-proof and contextual no-fit reasons without humiliating the artist or fabricating demand.

Fields: action type, optional structured reason, opportunity context, share permission and release version.

Artist display: only bounded, privacy-safe observations when policy and minimum aggregation permit. At small N show absolute statements without identity or percentages.

  

6\. PERSONA 3 — SOURCE CONFIRMER

  

SC1 — Bounded Source Confirmation

Routes: /source-confirmation/:secureId, /review, /result and /withdraw.

Trigger: an authorized participant creates a bounded request concerning one exact statement, artist and event.

Job: review that statement and confirm, correct, decline or state that it cannot be assessed.

Reads: minimum statement context, requester, proposed public wording/use, token purpose and expiry.

Writes: idempotent response, correction, timestamp, attribution choice and later withdrawal state.

Permission: the secure token grants access only to the named task. Progressive identity assurance may be required for risk exceptions; no account is required for the ordinary bounded task.

Rules: public attribution is a separate choice; no response is a general endorsement or future-booking implication; no response publishes automatically.

States: loading; invalid token; open; submitting; submitted; used; expired; requester-revoked; corrected; declined; cannot assess; withdrawn; network failure and retry.

Recovery: a failed submit preserves the selected response and correction; retry cannot create duplicate responses. Expired or revoked tasks show a safe result without unrelated artist information.

Handoff: confirmation may return the Claim to artist review. Correction or withdrawal creates Claim and PassportVersion remediation; an immutable published version is never silently rewritten.

Analytics: source\_confirmation\_opened; source\_confirmation\_submitted; source\_confirmation\_corrected; source\_confirmation\_declined; source\_confirmation\_cannot\_assess; source\_confirmation\_withdrawn; source\_confirmation\_retry.

Acceptance: minimum disclosure; clear receipt; later correction/withdrawal; idempotent response; no unrelated artist information; no general endorsement.

Reads: artists covered by ArtistAccess, authorized request counts and entitlement state.

Precondition: the acting Person has the required organization capability; the invitation names ArtistAccess as its purpose.

Writes: invitation and ArtistAccess with artist, organization where applicable, scope, status, start/end, grantor, acceptance, revocation, suspension and dispute state.

Rules: OrganizationMembership invitation and ArtistAccess invitation are separate. Accepting one never creates the other. Manager assistance does not include publication authority unless an explicit approved scope grants it.

States: draft; sent; accepted; declined; expired; revoked; suspended; disputed; wrong recipient; duplicate invitation; network failure and retry.

Recovery: invalid or expired invitation returns to a safe context and may allow a new request; revoked access takes immediate effect across active sessions without revealing future artist data.

Analytics: artist\_access\_invited; artist\_access\_accepted; artist\_access\_declined; artist\_access\_expired; artist\_access\_revoked; artist\_access\_suspended; artist\_access\_disputed.

Acceptance: paying agency does not erase artist control; organization membership alone cannot read artist-private data; revoked access cannot read future data.

  

AG3 — Requests Inbox

Interpretation rules: screen views, meaningful content visibility, saves, forwards, praise, future-fit, missing-proof feedback, commercial requests, confirmation, payment evidence and entitlement activation are separate outcomes. None is inferred from another.

Analytics never receive raw evidence, exact confidential values, community contacts, private notes or message content.

Initial small-N validation reports absolute counts. Percentages are secondary only after the experiment plan declares a valid denominator.

  

11\. DEFINITION OF DONE

A screen is design-done only when its job, context, fields, visibility, permissions, consent or other approved lawful-basis dependency, state model, analytics, mobile Hebrew RTL and English LTR parity, state-preserving locale switching, accessibility and recovery are specified; current Product Language and components are used; no firewall defect exists; and implementation is reviewed in the declared environment.

A complete beta journey additionally requires persisted data, correct positive and negative access tests, real recovery, audit where applicable and a written Design QA verdict.

14\. CROSS-ANCHOR LOW-FIDELITY ACCEPTANCE

Context: active persona, role, organization and artist are explicit where relevant.

Primary decision: one dominant action per task state or bounded panel. Operational surfaces may expose multiple actions when hierarchy clearly distinguishes primary, secondary and utility actions.

Empty state: explains what is absent, why it matters and the next permitted action.

Loading: preserves hierarchy and never implies final evidence.

Error: preserves entered data and provides retry, replacement or alternate safe continuation.

Stale state: displays reviewed date and a refresh route.

Forbidden/revoked: reveals no private record existence or metadata.

Public/private separation: private Radar status, gaps, exact values and recommendations cannot mount in Passport output.

Localization and responsive parity: Artist and Source Confirmer tasks complete at 360 px in every activated locale, with Hebrew RTL and English/Russian/German LTR; Passport reads and acts at 360 px in every activated locale; agency tables collapse to labelled cards without losing meaning or actions. 15\. PRODUCT CONTRACT CLOSURE — WAVE 1

Independent and represented artist contexts are distinct. Independent artists control self-owned records directly. Represented artists retain identity and publication control; manager or agency assistance requires active ArtistAccess with explicit scope.

  

Invitation purposes are distinct: account/beta, OrganizationMembership and ArtistAccess. Acceptance creates only the named record. OrganizationMembership and ArtistAccess are always evaluated separately.

  

Professional persistence is distinct: SavedPassport is private workspace state; ProfessionalReaction records an explicit action; AvailabilityRequest records only check-availability or price/details intent. No action creates a booking or post-booking workflow.

  

Permission and recovery are action-time requirements. Recoverable validation, network and delivery failures preserve entered input. Expired, revoked, suspended, disputed, deleted and withdrawn states return to the nearest lawful context without leaking private record existence.

  

Publication readiness has two independent dimensions: evidence readiness under the minimum credible-Passport rule, and commercial publication permission under the active OfferVersion where configured. Payment never changes Claim support or method label.
